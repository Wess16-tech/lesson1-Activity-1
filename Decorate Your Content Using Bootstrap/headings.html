MICROPLASTICS

Hidden Enviromental Threat

Microplastics are fragments of any type of plastic less than 5 mm (0.20 in) in length, according to the U.S. (NOAA) and the European Chemicals Agency. They cause pollution by entering natural ecosystems from a variety of sources, including cosmetics, clothing, food packaging, and industrial processes.

When plastics do break down they don`t biodegrade; rather, they break into increasingly smaller pieces, many of which end up in the oceans as microplastics that harm aquatic life and birds.

By David Suzuki
